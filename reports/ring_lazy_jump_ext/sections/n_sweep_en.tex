\section{Fix \texorpdfstring{$\beta=0.01$}{beta=0.01} and sweep \texorpdfstring{$N$}{N} (exact AW)}
Using the automatically selected $\beta_\ast=0.01$ and fixing $q=2/3$, $\rho=1$, and \texttt{lazy\_selfloop}, we sweep even $N$ and track peak locations/heights and tail decay.
Figure~\ref{fig:n-sweep-peaks-en} shows that $t_2$ shifts right as $N$ grows (a slower second time scale), while $h_2$ decreases; the tail rate $\gamma$ decreases with $N$, indicating a heavier tail, and the trend is summarized in Figure~\ref{fig:n-sweep-gamma-en}.
