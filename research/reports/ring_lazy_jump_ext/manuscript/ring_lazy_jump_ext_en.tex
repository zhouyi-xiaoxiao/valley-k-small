\section*{Key conclusions and key figures}
With $N=100,q=2/3,\rho=1$ and only $\beta$ varied, both peaks move to earlier times and the tail rate $\gamma$ increases with $\beta$;
at the same time, $K=4$ sustains a wider bimodal window and a deeper valley.

$\texttt{hv\_over\_max}\approx0.34$ for $K=2$ versus about $0.15$ for $K=4$, indicating a deeper valley and clearer separation for $K=4$;
at the same $\beta$, $K=4$ has earlier $(t_1,t_2)$ and larger $\gamma$, consistent with faster time scales and lighter tails.

MC classification confirms that jump-over appears only for $K=4$.
We sort trajectories by whether the shortcut is used at least once ($C0$ vs $C1p$) and whether a jump-over occurs ($J0$ vs $J1p$),
giving the four classes $\texttt{C0J0}$, $\texttt{C1pJ0}$, $\texttt{C0J1p}$, and $\texttt{C1pJ1p}$.
Under this lens, $K=2$ keeps $J$-related classes at zero across windows:
peak1 is essentially all $\texttt{C1pJ0}$, the valley is mostly $\texttt{C1pJ0}$ with a small $\texttt{C0J0}$ tail,
and peak2 is dominated by $\texttt{C0J0}$ with a smaller $\texttt{C1pJ0}$ share.
For $K=4$, peak1 splits into roughly two-thirds $\texttt{C1pJ0}$ and one-third $\texttt{C1pJ1p}$;
the valley shows a similar one-third $J1p$ share, while peak2 flips to $\texttt{C0}$-dominated with about a quarter $\texttt{C0J1p}$.
These $J1p$ paths correspond to delayed crossings without immediate absorption, and the curves vary only mildly with $\beta$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:mc-beta-classes-en}).

To choose a representative bimodal setting, we apply $t_2/t_1\ge10$, $\texttt{hv\_over\_max}\le0.6$,
and $\texttt{h2\_over\_h1}\ge0.05$, selecting the smallest $\beta$ that satisfies both $K=2$ and $K=4$; the scan yields $\beta_\ast=0.01$.

Fixing $\beta_\ast=0.01$ and sweeping $N$ shows $t_2$ shifting right, $h_2$ decreasing, and $\gamma$ decreasing (heavier tails),
while $t_1$ stays roughly stable; see Figures~\ref{fig:n-sweep-peaks-en} and~\ref{fig:n-sweep-gamma-en}.

The MC sweep over $N$ sharpens the same contrast.
For $K=2$, everything remains in $J0$: peak1 is locked to $\texttt{C1pJ0}$, the valley is mostly $\texttt{C1pJ0}$,
and peak2 mixes $\texttt{C0J0}$ with $\texttt{C1pJ0}$ as $N$ changes.
For $K=4$, once the valley/second peak are well formed, peak1 and the valley hold a stable $\sim$one-third $J1p$ share
(largely $\texttt{C1pJ1p}$), while peak2 stays $\texttt{C0}$-dominated with about a quarter $\texttt{C0J1p}$,
showing that jump-over contributions persist across $N$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:mc-n-classes-en}).
