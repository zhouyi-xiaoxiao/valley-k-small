\section{MC sweep: four classes vs \texorpdfstring{$N$}{N} (fixed \texorpdfstring{$\beta=0.01$}{beta=0.01})}
With $\beta=0.01$ fixed, we scan even $N$ and estimate class proportions within each window.
Recall: $C$ records whether the shortcut is used at least once ($C0$ vs $C1p$) and $J$ records whether a jump-over occurs ($J0$ vs $J1p$),
so the four classes are $\texttt{C0J0}$, $\texttt{C1pJ0}$, $\texttt{C0J1p}$, and $\texttt{C1pJ1p}$.
Figure~\ref{fig:mc-n-classes-en} summarizes the class proportions vs $N$.

\paragraph{Comparison highlights}
For $K=2$, $J$-related classes are essentially zero;
for $K=4$, $J$-related classes appear prominently in the valley and second-peak windows, reflecting jump-over contributions to delayed absorption.
