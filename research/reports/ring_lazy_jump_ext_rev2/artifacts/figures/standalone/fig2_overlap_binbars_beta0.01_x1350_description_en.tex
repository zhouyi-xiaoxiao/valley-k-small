\noindent
Figure~1 shows two stacked panels (top $K=2$, bottom $K=4$). In each panel the
first-passage-time distribution $f(t)$ is plotted (dark blue/black for $K=2$,
red for $K=4$), and three full-height stacked bars mark the peak1/valley/peak2
windows. The x-axis is discrete time $t=1..1350$ (cropped for readability while
still covering the second peak). For each $K$, the window centers
$(t_1,t_v,t_2)$ are detected from that panel's exact $f(t)$ and the half-width
is $\Delta=\max\{1,\lfloor 0.05(t_2-t_1)\rfloor\}$, so the bars align with each
panel's own peak/valley structure. Each bar spans $[t_L,t_R]$ and its colors
encode the four trajectory classes with heights equal to
$P(\text{class}\mid T\in\text{window})$ for that $K$:
\texttt{C0J0} = no shortcut and no jump-over,
\texttt{C1pJ0} = shortcut used at least once and no jump-over,
\texttt{C0J1p} = no shortcut and jump-over at least once,
\texttt{C1pJ1p} = shortcut used at least once and jump-over at least once.
Data come from \texttt{data/ft\_beta0.01.csv} and
\texttt{data/fig2\_bins\_bars\_beta0.01.json} with
$N=100$, $q=2/3$, $\beta=0.01$, $\rho=1.0$, lazy\_selfloop.
